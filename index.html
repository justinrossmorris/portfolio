About Me

I spent over a decade in academia studying how people argue, persuade, and make meaning. My Ph.D. in Philosophy from McMaster University focused on human dignity, specifically defending a concept that critics dismissed as useless. (Turns out, defending unpopular positions is good training for marketing.)

Before joining Insight, I consulted on ethics for the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, translating controversial but life-saving biotech research into policy recommendations. Then I discovered I could make a living writing about cloud infrastructure instead of moral philosophy. The pivot stuck.

Now I write for Fortune 500 executives. The CEO says she trusts me. After 127+ deliverables, I think I've earned it. My job is to make complex ideas land, whether that's a keynote for 1,300 salespeople or a two-minute video that closes a $19M deal.

I also taught Environmental Philosophy at McMaster. My students were kind enough to give me a median 9.5/10 on evaluations. One wrote: "Best philosophy course I have taken. Any other professor would not have done the content justice." The academic job market had other plans, but I still think about that classroom sometimes.

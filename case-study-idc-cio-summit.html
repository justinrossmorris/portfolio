1 Overview

Reem Gedeon, SVP & General Manager of Insight Canada, was invited to speak at the IDC CIO Summit. The audience: 46% C-level executives, 20% VPs, and 34% Directors, spanning financial services, healthcare, government, and mid-market companies.

The topic: Insight's 32-month AI transformation journey. The challenge: make a technical story about internal AI adoption (InsightGPT, governance frameworks, change management) compelling and actionable for CIOs evaluating their own AI strategies.

This wasn't a sales pitch. It was thought leadership, positioning Insight as the company that figured it out first and could show others the way.

2 Challenge

External conference keynotes have a different calculus than internal events:

  • •Skeptical audience: CIOs have heard every vendor claim. The content needed third-party validation, not marketing language.
  • •Technical depth vs. accessibility: The story involved AI infrastructure, governance models, and adoption metrics. It had to be sophisticated enough for technical leaders but clear enough for business executives.
  • •Diverse industries: Financial services (31%), healthcare and government (26%), and mid-size companies (42%) all have different AI concerns. One message wouldn't land for everyone.
  • •20 minutes, dense content: The story covered 32 months of transformation, multiple platforms, and a repeatable framework. Every minute had to count.

3 Approach

Research integration: To build credibility with a skeptical CIO audience, I anchored every major claim in third-party data. The presentation cited IDC ($10.30 ROI per $1 invested), Forrester (10-month payback), Gartner (65% of CIOs lack AI-ready data), McKinsey (85% of healthcare exploring AI), Gallup (19% industry average for AI adoption), and Bain (88% of transformations fail). These weren't decorative; they were structural.

The "Client Zero" narrative: Instead of leading with product, we led with vulnerability. Insight became Microsoft's first enterprise AI partner in 2022 and used itself as the lab. The message: "We made the mistakes so you don't have to." This reframed a vendor presentation as a peer sharing lessons learned.

Audience-segmented sections: I built three dedicated slides, one each for financial services CIOs, healthcare/government CIOs, and mid-size company leaders. Each addressed their specific concerns:

  • •Financial services: Privacy and compliance frameworks from day one. "Focus on the 'won't work for me' crowd first."
  • •Healthcare/Government: Reframed risk-aversion as a strength. "Slow and steady beats fast and chaotic."
  • •Mid-size companies: Agility over resources. "Don't copy enterprise playbooks. Write your own."

Strategic engagement break: At the 9-10 minute mark, I built in a live poll: "What's your biggest AI adoption barrier?" This wasn't filler. It let her acknowledge the room's concerns directly and pivot the back half of the talk to address them.

Stage direction layer: The speaker notes included energy cues (ENTHUSIASM, WARM CLOSE), physical direction (GESTURE to each component, MAKE EYE CONTACT during poll), and pacing markers (PAUSE before "sustainable," CLICK to transition). These helped her own the room, not just read the slides.

4 Sample Excerpt

Opening Hook

"When Microsoft asked us to be their first enterprise AI partner in 2022, there was no playbook.

So we wrote one.

32 months later, 14,000 employees in, 750,000 prompts a month: here's what worked and what didn't."

The Vulnerability Pivot

"Days 1 through 30: excitement and experimentation. Everyone's playing with it.

Days 30 through 60: 'This doesn't work for MY job.'

Sound familiar? 88% of transformations fail. Not because of the technology. Because of the culture."

The Close

"I'm not here to sell you anything. I'm here to share what we learned the hard way: the mistakes, the wins, the things that flopped.

Let's talk after. I'll buy the coffee."

5 The Deliverables

This project required a full thought leadership package:

  • •17-slide storyboard: Visual-by-visual breakdown with speaker notes, transitions, and timing marks.
  • •Full speaker notes: Detailed talking points for each slide, including energy cues, stage directions, and contingency prompts.
  • •Citation reference sheet: Source documentation for every external stat (IDC, Gartner, Forrester, McKinsey, Gallup, Bain) with page numbers and context.
  • •Backup Q&A prep: Anticipated questions on budget, timeline, team size, security, and resistance, with suggested responses.
  • •Audience-segmented messaging: Dedicated slides for financial services, healthcare/government, and mid-size company leaders.

6 Impact

The presentation positioned Insight as a peer. Leading with internal lessons rather than product capabilities earned credibility with an audience that's heard every sales pitch.

The "Client Zero" framing became a reusable asset. The narrative structure and research backing have since been adapted for other external speaking opportunities and sales enablement content.

More importantly: this was Reem's first major external thought leadership moment in her role as SVP Canada. The content had to make her look like the smartest person in the room on AI transformation. It did.

What This Demonstrates

  • ✓ Research integration: Building credibility through third-party data instead of marketing claims
  • ✓ Technical translation: Making complex AI infrastructure stories accessible to business executives
  • ✓ Audience segmentation: Tailoring content for distinct industry verticals within a single presentation
  • ✓ Thought leadership positioning: Framing a company story as peer insight rather than vendor pitch
